Hello, I'm Daniel!

In 2020, due to closures and restrictions caused by the Covid-19 global pandemic, I was sadly let go from my job as a poker dealer in Las Vegas, Nevada, USA. However, instead of waiting and hoping that my previous job would eventually come calling, I decided to use my free time to learn new skills online. I had always enjoyed working with computers so I began learning about business staples like Excel and PowerPoint. Eventually, my odyssey took me into the realm of web design and coding which culminated in a capstone project to design my own webpage; the very page you are visiting right now!

Experience

Red Rock Casino Resort & Spa

2006-2020
  • Supervisor
  • Tournament Director
  • Poker Dealer
  • Cashier

Though I initially applied to be a poker dealer, in May 2006 I was hired as a part-time cashier in the poker room. After about a month, I became the full-time swing shift cashier and I held that position until March of 2007 when I was promoted to poker dealer. I spent the next 13 years perfecting my craft and getting to know many of our regular customers. Beginning in 2020, I began officially training to become a supervisor and tournament director. My training was completed in March, one week before the Red Rock closed.

Education

University of California Los Angeles

2000-2002
2003-2006

I came from a long line of UCLA Bruins so when it came time to apply to colleges, I only applied to one. To my great delight, I was accepted into my dream school to play baseball. Unfortunately, I injured my shoulder during my senior year of high school and despite a full summer of very strenuous rehab, I reinjured it during my first year at UCLA. Without baseball, I began to feel lost and decided to take a one year break after my second year. In 2003, I returned to UCLA and joined the Zeta Psi Fraternity of North America. I focused my studies on history and spent much of my time playing poker and participating in fraternity activities. I left UCLA in 2006 and immediately moved to Las Vegas with the hope of becoming a poker dealer.

Glendora High School

1996-2000

I left middle school as a scrawny, bullied kid, but arrived at high school nearly a foot taller and ready for a new beginning. I played both football and baseball my freshman year but after that, I decided to stick with only baseball. By my senior year, I was getting straight As and I was not only playing on the school varsity team but also on the Pasadena Red Birds, a travelling scout team affiliated with the St. Louis Cardinals.
