Hello, I'm Daniel!

In 2020, due to closures and restrictions caused by the Covid-19 global pandemic, I was sadly let go from my job as a poker dealer in Las Vegas, Nevada, USA. However, instead of waiting and hoping that my previous job would eventually come calling, I decided to use my free time to learn new skills online. I had always enjoyed working with computers so I began learning about business staples like Excel and PowerPoint. Eventually, my odyssey took me into the realm of web design and coding which culminated in a capstone project to design my own webpage; the very page you are visiting right now!

*** To Those Grading This Project ***

You should notice that this webpage responds to a variety of screen sizes and orientations. On mobile devices all information is displayed in a single column with a sticky dropdown navigation bar at the top and different colored sections. However, turning the device sideways into "landscape" view, will change the navigation bar from a dropdown menu to an inline list. Viewing the page on a desktop or laptop will display the navigation down the left side of the screen with added icons. The title is replaced with an animated gif of my creation and items on the main section of the page will now appear in more columns, as can be seen in the Experience lists, the Skills links, and the High/Low game. I also replaced the solid lines under the section headers with a page break image.

Regardless of your viewing device, a carousel of pictures appears at the top of the main page. Use the left and right arrows to see the different pictures. All links have some sort of hover effect and Javascript was also added to give a smooth scroll when clicking on one of the navigation links. Also using Javascript, hovering over the pictures at the top of the Experience, Education, and For Fun sections will display a new picture. Clicking on any of the links in the Skills section will display the corresponding certificate in a "Light Box". In the For Fun section, you will see a game I created using Javascript with numerous steps taken to validate the input. Give it a try and good luck! Lastly, I created a form where you can contact me if you have any questions.

Please take the time to run the page through W3C validation as well as Webaim. I made sure to carefully craft this page with semantics and accessability in mind. It took 216 lines of HTML, 943 lines of CSS styling, and 169 lines of Javascript code to make this page so please take your time and explore. Thank you very much for your time and effort!
